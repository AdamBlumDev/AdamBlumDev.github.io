The Fu­ture of Web Ty­pog­ra­phy

  • Ex­ter­nal Me­dia Ci­ta­tions

As it cur­rently stands, qual­ity ty­pog­ra­phy on the Web is nearly im­pos­si­ble to achieve. We are see­ing some good ad­di­tions com­ing in the fu­ture, how­ever:

  • Hy­phen­ation through CSS1 is fi­nally in the works, and it can’t come soon enough. The rag of an av­er­age para­graph on the Web is dis­tract­ingly un­even. They are the foun­da­tion of our text con­tent, and yet they’re un­ac­cept­able. The only cur­rent cross-browser so­lu­tion, in­sert­ing soft hy­phens, cre­ates more prob­lems than it solves: they ruin ac­ces­si­bil­ity, per­sist af­ter copy-past­ing, and add sig­nif­icant weight to text-heavy pages.

  • Hang­ing punc­tu­ation2 will fur­ther sculpt the newly-minted text blocks, by pulling punc­tu­ation to the mar­gins.

  • Coun­ter Styles3 will al­low one to de­fine cus­tom coun­ters for list items (① ② ③, “First” “Sec­ond” “Third”), ref­er­ence marks (*, †, ‡, §, ‖, ¶), and other in­cre­ment­ing el­ements.

Vari­able Fonts don’t seem ap­peal­ing to me. I saw no need for con­tin­uously vari­able font weights, and few of the de­mos4 did any more than that. Matthew But­ter­ick has a very de­tailed ar­ti­cle5 ex­plain­ing just how use­less they are. How­ever, us­ing vari­able fonts for op­ti­cal siz­ing could be an in­ter­est­ing idea, as­sum­ing type de­sign­ers could de­sign them ef­fec­tively.

Ex­ter­nal Me­dia Ci­ta­tions

When it comes to cit­ing sources, the <cite> el­ement is about all we have at our dis­posal. It’s strange to me that there is no way to ref­er­ence other me­dia via an ex­ter­nal file à la Bib­TeX6. Imag­ine be­ing able to load a ref­er­ence file via <meta> tag, with CSS-like def­ini­tions:

@book ref&shy;er&shy;ence_name {
    ti&shy;tle: "The Book Ti&shy;tle";
    au&shy;thor: "John Smith";
    pub&shy;lisher: "Pub&shy;lisher";
    year: "2004";
}

It could then be used in HTML to gen­er­ate a for­mat­ted ci­ta­tion. This:

<ref value="reference_name"/>

Could be ren­dered in the DOM as a <cite> el­ement, or maybe some­thing en­tirely dif­fer­ent.
