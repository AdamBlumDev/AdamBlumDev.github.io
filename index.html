The Sound

Rich jazz harmony with a funky groove. Counter-culture, class-conscious hip-hop lyricism. Desires and emotions made manifest through sound. We have an obligation to articulate a moral vision derived from the unconscious.

There are too many musical influences to list, but here’s a sampling off the top of my head: John Coltrane, Ornette Coleman, Pink Floyd, Parliament/Funkadelic, Jimi Hendrix, James Brown, The Grateful Dead, The Roots, A Tribe Called Quest, Flying Lotus, Shpongle, Noisia, Mac Miller.

Our reality is an expression of the cosmic consensus: that humankind is good, and all are equal. The power of music to inspire others and create community cannot be overstated — we can cut through the miasma of today’s isolationist, hyper-capitalist existence and catalyze meaningful social change.
